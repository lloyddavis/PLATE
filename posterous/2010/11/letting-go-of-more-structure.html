Letting go of more structure

November 16 2010, 7:18 AM  by Lloyd Davis
I've had some conversations in the last few days that have encouraged me to think more about minimising structure. 

This is a recurring theme in my work.  With #tuttle we ended up with it's Friday at 10am at the same place each week and I will show up so that there's at least one person for someone to talk to.  And it worked - it seemed like far too flimsy a proposition, but it gave the space for amazing things to happen.  So the question is always "How little stucture do you need for something interesting or useful to happen? Is there anything you can take away without the whole thing collapsing?"

I come back to thinking about this adventure as a minimal story, with a beginning, middle and an end.  The beginning is that I fly from London to somewhere on the West Coast.  The middle is that I travel across the USA somehow, including spending some time at SXSWi and meeting up with at least some of the people I met last year.  The end is that I fly home from the East Coast.

What that does is to open up all sorts of possibilities for the middle, including involving people in decisions more, allowing myself to be taken places I wouldn't ordinarily go and do things I wouldn't ordinarily do.  The big difference is that I'm letting go of the train as the primary means of transport.  I like using public transport and it was good to see that it was doable via Amtrak last year, *and* I still want to do some of the journey that way, but I think there's also much to be said for me doing part of the journey by tweeting "I'm in Vegas, anyone heading in an easterly direction tomorrow and want a travel buddy?"  It also means I can take more of a zig-zag route and not exclude big bits of the country just because they're not served by Amtrak.

Or something.
