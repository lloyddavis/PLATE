Holiday?

February 23 2011, 8:48 AM  by Lloyd Davis

Yesterday, Emma (@hubmum) asked "is this a charity thing or fund my holiday?". Today Milo (@nero) said "Social media moron redux http://t.co/tH7OOfT Is this a spoof? The guy just wants you to pay for his holiday... What am I missing?"

I know that if someone's prepared to call me a moron, at least I'm doing something worth talking about.  On the other hand, I think it's an interesting reaction and worth answering.  

Do I just want you to pay for my holiday? No, course not. The simple answer, I gave to Emma on twitter yesterday is that it isn't a holiday at all.  "It's a performance, a writing and film-making project" and I'm using the tool of crowdfunding that is increasingly used as a way of people being able to make art projects happen by going straight to their audience beforehand without jumping through complicated funding hoops for big chunks of cash from big funding bodies or corporations.

I guess there are two bits that people have been interested in funding.  The first is the performance.  They'll enjoy watching me make my way coast-to-coast, seeing what happens, where I go, what I learn, being part of the adventure without having to go and do it themselves.

Then there's the writing and film-making - people want  to hear the whole story with some reflection and analysis - some people are particularly interested in the social capital angle, understanding what the value is of working in this way - some people are just taken with hearing a hero's journey played out by someone they know.

That's why I think people are supporting my work, but I'd be fascinated to hear more from those who've already  contributed and anyone who's wavering too.

For example if you were putting this forward as a documentary where people could invest and get a return on any profits that came from the film, then I'm sure nobody would have an issue. They would take a calculated gamble and importantly they would have the chance of directly benefiting from the trip, i.e. Capitalism.

    Personally I find it fascinating, you're a social artist with a (positive) intangible output that's hard to see or quantify, living in a capitalist society that trades money for value. Maybe the tension here is because it's not very clear what the value exchange is for the patron or if there is any at all.

    Some people need a return (so they won't get what you're up to or be negative), some people don't mind a deferred return (it might make them feel good, entertained, or create +ve karma or serendipity), some people really don't care and are purely generous (if you believe these people exist!).

    Crowdfunding like Kickstarter.com usually states what's in it for the crowdfunder so I'm not surprised that you are getting grief from folks who can't see any return and would rather spend their money taking themselves off somewhere nice.

    Tricky one and would be interested to hear what others think.

    Keep pushing the boundaries though Lloyd and ignore the detractors, they are just jealous! :D
