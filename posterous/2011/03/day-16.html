Day 16

March 22 2011, 1:42 PM  by Lloyd Davis

For the first time in a few days there was no need to get downtown before noon.  Sarah was leaving for a conference in DC so we stayed at home, I did some much-needed laundry and took it fairly easy for once.

Phil needed to go in and do a last few interviews so Sarah drove me downtown after during lunch (drive-thru burgers - yum) and I got out and said goodbye while Phil got in and they both went off to the airport.

I'd become very accustomed to living with Sarah in just 5 days and hanging out with Phil a lot. I had that slightly-on-my-own feeling again.

Soon though I met up with Nat and Julia and we wandered along 6th Street, hung out in The Hideout for a cup of tea, wandered back along 6th Street and up Red River to find a cab.  At last I got a real feel for what the music festival is like (at least in the afternoon, I can imagine what it gets like after dark...)

That night I got as far north out of Austin as I've been.  Julia and Nat had kindly offered me the sofa-bed in their suite for the night, as there was no train until the following day.  They also took me to dinner at the nearby Sam's Boat seafood place which Nat described as "a hand grenade of America going off in your face": TV screens with every sport you'd want; neon advertising for beer, beer and more beer; nautical paraphenalia in the middle of Texas; waitresses in white vests and denim shorts who write cute messages to y'all about coming back soon on your check.
